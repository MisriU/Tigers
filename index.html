Tigers

What is a Tiger?

The tiger (Panthera tigris) is the largest living cat species and a member of the genus Panthera. It is most recognisable for its dark vertical stripes on orange fur with a white underside. Tigers are carnivorous mammals, and they mostly eat large prey like deer, wild boar and even elephant calves.

Fun Facts

1) Tigers are the largest wild cats in the world. 2) Tigers are carnivores, eating only meat. 3) Tigers are solitary hunters, and generally search for food alone at night. 4) Tigers are good swimmers! 5) A tiger's roar can be heard as far as three kilometres away.



Family

The tiger is a part of the Felidae family

Tiger, (Panthera tigris), largest member of the cat family (Felidae), rivaled only by the lion (Panthera leo) in strength and ferocity.

Diet

Diet: Tigers are carnivores and will eat whatever they can catch including fish, crocodiles and fowl, with the most common larger prey being wild pigs and deer. In the wild: The tiger is a solitary animal.
